=====================  Hazards Identification  =====================

Routes of Entry: Inhalation:YES  Skin:YES  Ingestion:YES
Health Hazards Acute and Chronic:EYES: SEVERE IRRITANT, CAUSING BLURRED
    VISION. SKIN: PROLONGED/REPEATED CONTACT CAN CAUSE MODERATE
    IRRITATION/DERMATITIS. INHALATION: IRRITATION OF NOSE/THROAT,
    ASPHYXIATION. INGESTION: ASPIRATION HAZ ARD. REPEATED/PROLONGED
    OVEREXPOSURE TO SOLVENTS MAY CAUSE PERMANENT BRAIN & NERVOUS SYSTEM
    DAMAGE. INTENTIONAL MISUSE BY DELIBERATELY CONCENTRATING & INHALING
    THE PRODUCT MAY BE HARMFUL/FATAL. INGEST ION: MINUTE AMOUNTS
    ASPIRATED MAY CAUSE MILD TO SEVERE PULMONARY INJURY & POSSIBLY
    DEATH.
Effects of Overexposure:IRRITATION, REDNESS, TEARING,BLURRED VISION,
    DEFATTING OF SKIN, DIZZINESS, WEAKNESS, FATIGUE, NAUSEA, HEADACHE,
    UNCONSCIOUSNESS, ASPHYXIATION, VOMITING
Medical Cond Aggravated by Exposure:PERSONS W/PRE-EXISTING CNS DISEASE,
    SKIN DISORDORS/CHRONIC RESPIRATORY DISEASES.

=======================  First Aid Measures  =======================

First Aid:EYES: FLUSH W/LARGE AMOUNTS OF WATER FOR 15 MINS. SKIN:
    THOROUGHLY WASH W/SOAP & WATER. INHALATION: REMOVE TO FRESH AIR. IF
    BREATHING IS DIFFICULT, ADMINISTER OXYGEN. IF BREATHING HAS
    STOPPED, GIVE CP R. KEEP PERSON WARM & QUIET. INGESTION: DON'T
    INDUCE VOMITING. OBTAIN MEDICAL ATTENTION IN ALL CASES. NOTE TO
    PHYSICIAN: PERFORM GASTRIC LAVAGE IAW/PROCEDURES FOR INGESTION OF
    PETROLEUM PRODUCTS.

=====================  Fire Fighting Measures  =====================

Flash Point Method:TCC
Flash Point:=39.4C, 103.F
Autoignition Temp:>204.4C, 400.F
Lower Limits:0.9
Upper Limits:6
Extinguishing Media:DRY CHEMICAL, CO/UNIVERSAL TYPE FOAM, WATER FOG MAY
    BE EFFECTIVE BUT MAY CAUSE FROTHING. DON'T USE WATER.
Fire Fighting Procedures:AS A PRECAUTION, EXPOSURE TO VAPORS, LIQUIDS,
    MISTS/FUMES SHOULD BE MINIMIZED.
Unusual Fire/Explosion Hazard:UNDER FIRE CONDITIONS MAY FORM TOXIC
    MATERIALS SUCH AS CO2, CO & NITROGEN OXIDES. PETROLEUM BASED
    COMPOUNDS CAN FLOAT ON WATER.

==================  Accidental Release Measures  ==================

Spill Release Procedures:SHUT OFF & ELIMINATE ALL IGNITION SOURCES.
    KEEP PEOPLE AWAY. RECOVER FREE PRODUCT. ADD SAND, EARTH/OTHER
    ABSORBENT TO AREA. MINIMIZE SKIN CONTACT & BREATHING OF VAPORS.
    VENTILATE CONFINED SPACES. OPEN  ALL WINDOWS & DOORS. KEEP PRODUCT
    OUT OF SEWERS & WATER COURSES BY DIKING/IMPOUNDING. ADVISE
    AUTHORITIES IF PRODUCT HAS ENTERED/MAY ENTER SEWERS/WATER
    COURSES/EXTENSIVE LAND AREAS.

======================	Handling and Storage  ======================

Handling and Storage Precautions:KEEP CONTAINERS TIGHTLY CLOSED. KEEP
    CONTAINERS COOL, DRY & AWAY FROM SOURCES OF IGNITION. USE
    W/ADEQUATE VENTILATION. MATERIAL REQUIRES ELECTRICAL GROUNDING
    DURING MATERIAL TRANSFER PROCESS. ALL ELEC TRICAL EQUIPMENT IN
    STORAGE/HANDLING AREAS SHOULD BE INSTALLED PER NFPA REQUIREMENTS.
Other Precautions:EMPTY CONTAINERS RETAIN RESIDUE, LIQUID/VAPOR & CAN
    BE DANGEROUS. DON'T
    PRESSURIZE/CUT/WELD/BRAZE/SOLDER/DRILL/GRIND/EXPOSURE SUCH
    CONTAINERS TO HEAT/FLAME/SPARKS/OTHER SOURCES OF IGNITION. THEY MAY
    E XPLODE, CAUSE INJURY/DEATH. DON'T ATTEMPT TO CLEAN SINCE RESIDUE
    IS DIFFICULT TO REMOVE.

=============  Exposure Controls/Personal Protection  =============

Respiratory Protection:USE SUPPLIED AIR RESPIRATORY PROTECTION IN
    CONFINED/ENCLOSED SPACES, IF NEEDED.
Ventilation:SUFFICIENT TO PREVENT EXCEEDING RECOMMENDED EXPOSURE

    EXPLOSION PROOF EQUIPMENT.
Protective Gloves:CHEMICAL RESISTANT IF NEEDED, TO AVOID
