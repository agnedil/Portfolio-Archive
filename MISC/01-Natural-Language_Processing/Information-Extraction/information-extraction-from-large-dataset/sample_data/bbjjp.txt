=====================  Hazards Identification  =====================

Effects of Overexposure:MUCOUS MEMBRANE IRRITATION & TIGHTNESS OF THE
    CHEST. RESPIRATORY TRACT IRRIT, COUGHING, HEADACHE.

=======================  First Aid Measures  =======================

First Aid:EYES: FLUSH W/WATER FOR 15 MIN. CONSULT PHYS. SKIN: WASH
    W/WATER & TINCTURE OF GREEN SOAP. INHALATION: REMOVE TO
    UNCONTAMINATED AREA, ADMINISTER OXYGEN IF NEEDED. INGESTION: DON'T
    INDUCE VOMITING, GIV E MILK OR WATER AND GET MEDICAL HELP.

=====================  Fire Fighting Measures  =====================

Flash Point:270F, C.O.C.
Extinguishing Media:WATER FOG, CO2, DRY CHEMICAL
Fire Fighting Procedures:PROTECT AGAINST TDI/NITROGEN DIOXIDE VAPORS
    WITH SCBA.A
Unusual Fire/Explosion Hazard:AVOID WATER CONTAMINATION IN CLOSED
    CONTAINERS OR CONFINED SPACES; CO GAS WILL BE GENERATED.

==================  Accidental Release Measures  ==================

Spill Release Procedures:SPILLED CHEMICALS SHOULD BE COVERED WITH
    SAWDUST & TREATED WITH DILUTE AMMONIUM HYDRIDE OR ALCOHOL & WATER
    IN AN OPEN CONTAINER BEFORE DISPOSAL.

======================	Handling and Storage  ======================

Handling and Storage Precautions:MUST BE STORED IN TIGHTLY CLOSED
    CONTAINERS AND PROTECTED FROM MOISTURE. AVOID TEMPERATURES >9 (32C)
    OR <70F (21C.)
Other Precautions:THIS PRODUCT REACTS WITH WATER & OTHER SUBSTANCES TO
    PROVIDE CO2 GAS WHICH CAN CAUSE SEALED CONTAINERS TO EXPAND, &
    POSSIBLYUPTURE. DON'T RESEAL CONTAINER IF CONTAMINATION IS
    SUSPECTED.
